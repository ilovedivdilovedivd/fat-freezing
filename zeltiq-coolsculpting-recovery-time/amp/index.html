Zeltiq Coolsculpting Recovery Time

One of the great things about CoolSculpting is there are no restrictions following treatment, and the downtime is minimal.

Most commonly, patients may experience minor bruising, swelling, cramping, or altered sensation for a few days following treatment.

This is minor and rarely interferes with daily activities. Rarely (less than 5% of the time), a side effect called late onset pain will develop a few days after treatment, that can last up to two weeks.

This is typically treated with prescription medications.

Your provider should address all possible side effects in detail and provide thorough post procedure instructions. (Kent V. Hasen, MD, Naples Plastic Surgeon)

The best part about CoolSculpting is that there is no downtime following the procedure. Patients can resume their regular activities immediately following the procedure, even return to the gym the same day.

It Is Not Uncommon For The Treated Area To Bruise

A patient may experience some minor redness, minimal swelling and bruising which will disappear within a week.

Numbness can persist up to one month but does not effect daily activities. (Alan N. Larsen, MD, Atlanta Plastic Surgeon)

While there is no specific downtime, you can be swollen for a week or two and numb in the areas treated for a few weeks.

Beyond that, there is a risk of late-onset pain mostly if treated in the abdominal area that can start on day 5-7 after treatment and last up to 14 days after treatment.

This is treated with medication and compression and is always self-limited and doesn’t happen to everyone. (Sheila S. Nazarian, MD, Beverly Hills Plastic Surgeon)

Since CoolSculpting is not a surgical procedure, you can resume normal activity after your CoolScultpting treatment.

We offer a complimentary consultation to determine if you are a good candidate for the procedure. (Lenore Sikorski, MD, Orange County Dermatologist)

Recovery after Coolscultping

You can consider recovery as follows:

  1. You won’t hurt yourself if you went and did an ‘all out’ workout right after the procedure. You may feel slightly or moderately uncomfortable, but you won’t hurt yourself for doing this.
  2. Discomfort: This varies by individual and by treated body area. The abdominal area is usually the treatment where patients may experience some variation of discomfort that can linger between 1-2 weeks. (Young R. Cho, MD, PhD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

Recovery post coolsculpting

Patients can resume activities immediately post treatment. We have had patients who have a coolsculpting treatment in the morning, and went to the gym in the afternoon. Patients may experience tenderness, swelling, and bruising post treatment but actual pain is in less than 3% of the cases nationwide. (Kris M. Reddy, MD, FACS, West Palm Beach Plastic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting and Recovery

Most people require 2-4 weeks where they need to take it easy, as there is swelling, bruising and irritation at the site. Some get a tingling sensation due to the nerves in the area being irritated.

All this resolves and the results are seen in 3 months. (Jason Emer, MD, Los Angeles Dermatologic Surgeon)

Getting CoolSculpting and want to know how long the recovery is

Fortunately, recovery following Cool Sculpting is minimal. Some patients do experience a moderate amount of swelling. Occasionally, a minimal amount of bruising may occur. Other patients report numbness in the area treated which may persist for 4-6 weeks.

The good news is that you can return to your regular activities immediately following the procedure. (R. Stewart Collins, MD, Raleigh-Durham Plastic Surgeon)
