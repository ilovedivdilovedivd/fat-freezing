Coolsculpting for inner thighs

The results from Coolsculpting are dependent on a few factors. The areas treated should be large enough, but also pliable enough to fit into the Coolsculpting applicator well. It is not just a question of attaching to a site but also being able to get a sufficient amount of fat drawn into the chamber.

Secondly, Coolsculpting has its best results with patients that have specific areas of stubborn fat and are close to their goal weight. If a patient is overweight, results from Coolsculpting will be very limited and difficult to appreciate.

It is always best to get a face to face consult to get the most accurate assessment and decide if Coolsculpting would be the right procedure for you and give you a result you would be pleased with. (Grant Stevens, MD, Los Angeles Plastic Surgeon)

How to Address Cellulite in Thighs

I can only advise that liposuction works best in areas where there is still ample skin elasticity to effect sufficient retraction once the fat is removed.

For that, a better option might be Z-Wave therapy, which stimulates the shrinking of circumference by breaking down cellulite’s fibrous connective tissue so that the fat cells can release their fatty acids. In short, the fat cells deflate.

To complement this treatment, I would combine it with Carboxy Therapy. This procedure calls for the injection of carbon dioxide gas just below the skin’s surface. The CO2 stimulates circulation and oxygenation to further shrink the subcutaneous tissue and smooth out the skin’s surface. (Hal Michael Bass, MD, Miami Plastic Surgeon)

Swelling after Coolsculpting

Swelling on inner thighs one week after Coolsculpting is completely normal. This is actually very good news. Swelling means that you have initiated an inflammatory response. The inflammatory response is what is going to get rid of up to 25% of your fats cells in this area.

The swelling will subside over the next few weeks. (Stephanie Martin, MD, Atlanta Orthopaedic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting works through cryolipolysis, the process takes at least a week to complete. Most patient may still feel somewhat swallon 3 weeks post treatment. You usually will notice the swelling and weird skin feeling gone by the end of 4th weeks and see the good results showing off after a month.

Best results can be seen through second to third months after treatment. (Arash G. Pasha, MD, Kissimmee Physician)
