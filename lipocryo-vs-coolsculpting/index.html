How does LipoCryo work?

A special sensor is placed on the selected area, to combine the vacuum action to cover the subcutaneous tissue and selective action of the cryotechnology to cool it. Fat cells (adipocytes) in the subcutaneous tissue (under the dermis) are in most cells saturated with fatty acids, and therefore they are especially sensitive to cold;
in contrast, contiguous elements such as blood vessels, peripheral nervous system, melanocytes, fibroblasts or unsaturated fat cells are much less sensitive to cold. This characteristic has a certain effect on the removal of adipocytes without damaging the surrounding tissues.

How are fat cells removed?

The process of removal of isolated adipocytes as a result of the action of LipoCryo is performed by crystallization, followed by apoptosis and subsequent dissolution of cells with a gradual release of fats over a period of 2 to 6 weeks.

A step-by-step process

LipoCryo provides a cold to the adipose tissue (the process of extracting heat from the tissue) with a selective and controlled procedure to reach a temperature of less than 10 ° C at the adipocyte level for a predetermined time.

The maximum inflammatory process occurs approximately 15 days after the session, when adipocytes are surrounded by lymphocytes, histiocytes and other mononuclear cells. At this time, the process of progressive reduction in the number of adipocytes and their size begins.

In the period from 15 to 30 days, the inflammation will lead to a phagocytic process: macrophages begin to wrap around and digest apoptotic adipocytes to facilitate their progressive removal from the body.
