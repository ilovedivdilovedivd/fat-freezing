1. What is cryolipolysis and fat traps?

Very important! Cryolipolysis is not a method for treating body obesity or weight loss, it is a method of reducing the characteristic local fatty deposits on the back, stomach, sides, buttocks.

The method is not applicable to the second chin and very rarely can be used on the shoulders for one simple reason – the effect should be on the fat thickness, and not on the muscles or flabby (atonic, dangling) skin. At the present time, manipulations of various sizes for other (small) areas are being tested clinically.

Fat traps are places on the human body, where, depending on sex and genetics, fat can accumulate. Nobody knows why and why some women, for example, have thick knees.

About fat traps you can say “The first to come – the last to leave”. Often appearing in the period of puberty, they do not depend on the total weight (ie can be and in thin people), and due to the features of metabolism in these places practically do not decrease: neither as a result of physical exercises nor diets.

Typically, several typical areas are identified that are most troubling, but from the point of view of anatomy and surgery, there are many more.

Coolsculpting is a method of reducing local fat deposits based on cooling of subcutaneous fat, as a result of which some of the adipocytes die naturally (apoptosis), which leads to a decrease in the volume of adipose tissue.

2. Who is the founder of the Coolsculpting?

The technique is based on fundamental scientific research of the properties of fat tissue conducted by scientists in different countries and the original developments of the scientific team led by Rox Anderson.

The first experiments began in the late 90s and (then another employee of the Wellman-center of photomedicine at the Masachutessa Hospital, a branch of Harvard University (USA)) Dieter Manstein brought this development to the stage of commercial application and considers himself the inventor of this method.

The impetus to the beginning of the work was the observation of pediatricians – in children abused constant sucking of fruit ice cream developed so-called cold panniculitis – inflammation of the subcutaneous fat in the mouth and cheeks. And in these places the thickness of subcutaneous fat was thinner.

Coolsculpting Employs Selective Cryolipolisis (Fat Freezing) Using A Set Of Plates To Reach Temperature Up To -10 Celsius

Zeltiq Aestetic Inc., USA, was established to promote the technology of cryolipolysis using venture capital. That’s how they formulated their credo. “Our mission is to create differentiated, clinically proven, safe, and beneficial solutions with one goal in mind: a cooler you.”

How Does CoolSculpting Work?

It was empirically established that with the volume cooling of adipose tissue in the interval 20-25 ° C and moderate hypoxia, only a part of the adipocytes diffuse “freezes.”

After “thawing” there is no necrosis, respectively – there is no excess of lipid levels in the blood, etc.

The skin, muscles, nerves or other tissues and organs do not suffer under the influence of such a temperature. It is believed that the program of apoptosis “turns on” in these frozen adipocytes.

The natural process of cell dying constantly occurring in our body can not be harmful. It is believed that about 5% of all cells in the body die daily.

Currently, among the non-invasive methods of reducing the volume of subcutaneous fat in the field of typical “fat traps,” the cryolipolysis technique has been recognized as one of the most selective and safe.

Coolsculpting Treatment Time Is Less Than 1 Hour

The first experiments were carried out on pigs (body temperature and pig biochemistry is the closest to man).

In 2008, the first work on this topic was published: “Selective Cryolysis: A Novel Method of Non-Invasive Fat Removal”, D. Manstein, MD; H. Laubach, MD; K. Watanabe, MD; W. Farinelli, MD; D. Zurakowski, MD; R. Rox Anderson, MD; Lasers in Surgery and Medicine – October 2008.

In 2009, the results obtained on volunteers among people were published.

In 2010, the FDA issued a permit to use the technique on the fat deposits of the back and sides and in May 2012 on the folds of the abdomen.
By the beginning of 2012, only 30000 procedures were performed on Zeltiq devices.

Not only for the purpose of diluting fat for its better aspiration, but also for increasing the percentage of death of fat cells among those remaining after aspiration and to enhance the production of collagen. Starvation diets, food supplements and a variety of drugs for weight loss is almost no effect on fat traps. If you apply them reasonably, i.e. do not bring yourself to an extreme degree of exhaustion (cachexia).

Cryolipolysis is a new method of selective reduction of local fat deposits. By scientific experiments and clinical observations it is proved – today it is the most specific and safe method of reducing local fat deposits based on the reduction in the number of fat cells.

Cryolipolysis scientific term, formed from the merger of two (three) words Cryo + Lipo + Lysis also wanted to be patented on behalf of the company Zeltiq.

But, in fact, formed from known words, it has already become a common noun and, according to the nature of its definition, can be used with respect to similar principles of influence – selective, non-invasive, controlled cooling of adipose tissue resulting in apoptosis of a part of adipocytes and a decrease in the volume of adipose tissue.
