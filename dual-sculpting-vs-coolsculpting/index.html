Dual Sculpting Vs Coolsculpting

DualSculpting™ is the most effective way to remove more fat cells in a shorter amount of time.

If you’re looking to maximize your results in a shorter amount of time, try to find a plastic surgeon in your area who offers DualSculpting™. There are two main advantages to this technique.

First, if you plan to have multiple areas treated, you can essentially cut your treatment time in half by having them both performed using separate machines at the same time. Secondly, treating the same session back-to-back can eliminate up to 60 percent of the targeted fat cells, as they are already numb from the previous session, making the second one more efficient. (Jennifer Harrington, MD, Minneapolis Plastic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting

The method of cryolipolysis allows to reduce the amount of fat deposits without penetration by tools under the skin of a person.

The procedure “CoolSculpting” has already proved effective in clinical trials. The importance of fat cells in the human body is quite large. They contribute to the human struggle with an unfavorable climate – both warm and cold (most often with it).Fat cells are very sensitive to low temperature, in contrast to those tissues that are next to them.

Coolsculpting means a method that allows you to reduce the amount of fat cells. You do not need to carry out operations with the skin incision – the cold reaches the cells that make up fat, causing them to die, broken into fragments. The resulting fragments are absorbed by macrophages of blood and lymph, as well as cells located near the tissues, and are then excreted through the liver.

This process allows you to destroy fragments without provoking an inflammation reaction. In other words, the procedure “CoolSculpting” means the destruction of fat cells by the cold factor. In the course of it, tissues and vessels are not damaged, and scarring is not formed.

Apoptosis is not synonymous with necrosis. After traumas of cells caused by mechanical action, scars remain after healing. Coolsculpting technology Zeltiq does not injure cells, and accordingly development of scar tissue is excluded.

Among the society there is a rumor that this procedure damages the liver and increases the lipid content in the blood. Studies have shown that dead cells removed through the liver do not harm the body.

Indication for the procedure

Before exposing your body to cryolipolysis, you should study all aspects of this manipulation well in order to be completely sure of its necessity.

Treatment of fat deposits by the Zeltiq device is performed with:

  • fat deposits on the abdomen and waist;
  • presence of a significant number of such formations on the back and sides;
  • flabbiness of the skin of hands and the “orange peel” on them;
  • fatty deposits in the area of the thighs.

In general, coolsculpting is indicated in pathologies:

  1. obesity associated with endocrine changes, when a person is gaining weight even from low-calorie food;
  2. with the same disease, based on psychological disorders, when any stress “inflames” the appetite;
  3. if weight gain occurs as a result of damage to one part of the brain (hypothalamus), which provokes an improper diet;
  4. when extra pounds appear with a sedentary lifestyle.

How many sessions are needed to achieve the result?

The number of procedures is determined on an individual basis, taking into account the initial condition of the skin, as well as the zone to be corrected, the weight of the patient. Usually you need 3-8 sessions.

For example, you can for 6-10 approaches to bring the body in the abdominal region to normal, remove fat deposits on your legs for 4-6 sessions. The break between sessions lasts 5-7 days. During this period, the patient must comply with increased drinking regimen, as well as diet. The course of cryolipolysis can be repeated, but not earlier than 4 months later.

A good effect and more pronounced results are obtained by combining this method with mesotherapy, RF-lifting, lymph drainage and vacuum-roller massage.
