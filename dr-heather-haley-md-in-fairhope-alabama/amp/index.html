How to get swelling to go down after IPL facial?
Swelling is common after IPL
Apr 13, 2018
Swelling is usually worse in the morning, so it can help to sleep with head elevated. Also antihistamines can help reduce the swelling, I recommend non sedating antihistamine, Zyrtec or Claritin, during the day and Benadryl at night.
How can I optimize my coolsculpting experience? (e.g. hydration level, electrolytic balance, fasting
Coolsculpting results and diet
Mar 1, 2014
I would advise to maintain your typical daily diet so the fat cells are stable. Coolsculpting is recommended for people who are close to their ideal weight and exercise regularly. It is not for weight loss. The fat cells will be destroyed by the freeze whether they are hydrated or not.
I am 60 years old, 5'1 and 132 lbs. Would I be a good candidate for Cool Sculpting?
Coolsculpting candidate
Mar 1, 2014
It is very hard to determine if people are good candidates for coolsculpting without actually seeing them. However, I have seen nice results in patients who have had liposuction in the past. I would encourage you to find a doctor in your area that has coolsculpting and seek a consultation. Often the consultation is free.
Does fat freeze really work?
Coolsculpting really works.
Dec 28, 2013
The ideal candidate for coolsculpting is someone who maintains a steady weight and exercises regularly. It does not reduce as much fat as liposuction, however it is noninvasive and very safe with no downtime. 20% reduction in the area treated is the expected result, some patients need to repeat the treatment to get further reduction.

Hyperpigmentation from Sclerotherapy?
Hyperpigmentation after sclerotherapy
Jul 21, 2013
Hyperpigmentation can occur after sclerotherapy. I see it more when hypertonic saline is used as the injectable. Since Asclera was approved by the FDA it is the agent that I use more often now. I like Asclera because it doesn't cause hyperpigmentation and it doesn't burn as it goes into the vessel. The darkening seen with saline usually occurs along the vessel but can also occur at the injection site. This can fade over time.
