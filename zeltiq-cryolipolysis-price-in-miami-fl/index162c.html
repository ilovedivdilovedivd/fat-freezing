What Is CoolSculpting and How Does It Work?

What is CoolSculpting?

CoolSculpting is a new procedure by Zeltiq that is perfect for people who are in good shape, but are looking to get rid of stubborn pockets of fat.

Targeted fat loss through exercise is obviously not possible, which is why a procedure like this is incredibly useful. It’s also great because it’s non-invasive, requires very little time, and has no known side-effects.

How Does CoolSculpting Work?

This is a procedure that was developed by Harvard scientists that reduces the temperature in certain areas of your body to a point at which the fat cells freeze.

This temperature is safe for your skin, nerves, muscle, blood vessels, and really everything but the fat cells.

When your fat cells freeze, they die, and when they die they are removed as a part of the process called apoptosis. Apoptosis is the name for natural cell death and the removal of those cells.

While CoolSculpting isn’t exactly natural, it forces the fat cells to undergo what your body thinks is a natural death. Your body will just remove those cells as naturally as it removes other dead cells, and they will be eliminated through your waste.

The CoolSculpting machine that is used is essentially a vacuum combined with cold packs.

The vacuum will suck in and hold onto the fatty area of your body, while the computer-controlled cold packs reduce the temperature to where it needs to be for the procedure to work.

It requires no surgery, and there’s no recovery time, but this procedure can only be done by properly licensed physicians so you know you’re getting the best care.

CoolSculpting Or Cryolipolysis Is A Non-surgical Treatment For Elimination Of Fat Cells

Another cool thing about CoolSculpting that it doesn’t take long at all. It takes anywhere from 30-90 minutes, which that with no recovery time, you can get it done on your lunch break.

Even if you go for just this one procedure, you will see noticeable results. It does take about 6 to 8 weeks for your body to eliminate the dead fat cells though, so you’ll have to be a patient patient.

There have also been no reported side effects of this procedure.

At most, some people have reported short-term loss or reduction of sensation in the area where the treatment was done. No one has reported this effect lasting long term though.
