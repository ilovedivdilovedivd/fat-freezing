What is Сoolsculpting?

People have known about the beneficial effect of cold and rejuvenating properties of ice for a long time. Suffice it to recall the grinding of snow after a bath and the use of ice cubes to preserve the tone, brightness and youth of the face. Studies have confirmed intuitive guesses.

It turned out that fat, which protects us from the cold, is very sensitive to it. Recently, a group of scientists has proved that the fatty layer on the face of women in frosty weather is significantly thinner. “Slimming with cold is not a novelty,” you can object. “Now you will not surprise anybody with cryosauna.” All right. Only in cryosauna, unfortunately, the effect of cold against fat is used up to a maximum of 5%.

Judge for yourself. To destroy the fat cell, it must be strongly cooled. But in the cryosauna, the fat tissue is cooled only from the outside, its uppermost layer. And from the inside, it is intensely heated by blood flow. The device for cryolipolysis is deprived of this lack.

Due to the presence of vacuum attachments, the fat fold is sucked into the applicator, the blood flow slows down and it effectively cools.

Only this device contains 4 vacuum nozzles of different sizes, which allows simultaneously correcting several zones, even small ones and difficult to access. Coolsculpting is not massage using vacuum, and it is indeed destroys fat once and for all.

When do I need cryolipolysis?

  • unaesthetic fatty deposits in the abdomen (especially the fat apron), waist, sides, buttocks,
  • on the back,
  • zones of inner and outer thighs (breeches),
  • fat over the knees,
  • the inner surface of the arms.
  • preparation for competitions (cryolipolysis allows you to reduce weight and emphasize the relief of muscles),
  • with ineffectiveness or the presence of contraindications to other methods of regulating the volume of adipose tissue,
  • to correct the figure and form a sexually attractive silhouette.

Contraindications to Coolsculpting

  • violation of blood circulation in the treatment zone,
  • take of anticoagulants,
  • hypersensitivity to cold (cold urticaria),
  • impaired skin sensitivity,
  • hernia,
  • diabetes,
  • neuropathic disorders,
  • pacemaker,
  • pregnancy and the period of breastfeeding,
  • fat folds less than 2.5 cm thick;
  • fresh injuries, wounds, scars and other skin lesions in the treatment area.

How many procedures will it take?

To achieve a pronounced and satisfactory result, 1 procedure is sufficient.

How often can I do cryolipolysis?

After 45-60 days, the procedure can be repeated locally to enhance the effect in problem areas.

How much does coolsculpting cost?

The price for the procedure is always determined individually and requires personal consultation of the dermatocosmetologist, as it depends on the number of treated zones and the number of necessary procedures.

How is the procedure performed?

After the examination, a special gel is applied to the area for cryolipolysis. Vacuum nozzle Coolsculpting, like a vacuum cleaner, draws in the fat fold and starts its cooling on both sides on the entire volume of adipose tissue. When the temperature reaches + 8 C, the fatty cell membrane dissolves, and its contents with blood and lymph currents are naturally eliminated.

The procedure lasts from 40 to 60 minutes and is absolutely comfortable.

During the procedure, you sit or lie, drink tea or read, watch a movie or listen to music, indulge yourself with an outgoing procedure or sleep lightly. After the end of the session, you can immediately return to your usual rhythm of life.
