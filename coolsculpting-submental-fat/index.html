Mini Cool Sculpting for chin

If you are decided between Cool Sculpting vs Kybella, it all depends on how much time and effort you are willing to put into the procedure. Cool Sculpting does not require anesthesia, you will not have an incision and there is no down time where activities are limited.

Kybella on the other hand has a series of needle sticks, a local anesthetic and more downtime that you will have to plan for. Kybella also has more swelling and hardness in the treated area than Cool Sculpting.

There also are other factors such as skin laxity, platysmal banding and jowls that will need to be considered before deciding that Cool Sculpting or Kybella is right for you. (Richard J. Wassermann, MD, MPH, Columbia Plastic Surgeon)

They are both great options and work well for the double chin, depending on where your fat is located. CoolSculpting involves no needles or bruising, and can treat the areas more lateral while Kybella treats the fat in the midline but cannot be injected more laterally.
