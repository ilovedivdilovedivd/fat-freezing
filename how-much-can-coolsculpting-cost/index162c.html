How much CoolSculpting costs

The Coolsculpting investment depends on a few factors.

  1. the area being treated,
  2. the number of applicators required to treat any given area,
  3. the type of applicators used,
  4. the number of treatments required to achieve the desired result.

That is why it is very difficult to give a precise investment. As an example, let’s say a patient wanted to treat their belly fat. They could be treating the lower abdomen (below the belly button), the mid abdomen (above the belly button), the upper abdomen (below rib cage).

They may require 1 treatment if they are closer to their ideal body weight, or 2 treatments if there is a more significant area to be treated. So you can see that the possibilities are many.

This patient may only treat the lower abdomen one time (an investment of approximately $1500) or may treat all three sections of the abdomen two times (an investment of approximately $5000).

Typically the more applicators you require for best results, the more the price per applicators gets reduced. Since there are many clinics offering CoolSculpting now, and the prices are more or less comparable, the most important decision is finding a clinic that has had the technology for some time, has experienced technicians that have the most up-to-date training and has the newest generation of applicators. (Manish Khanna, MD, FRCP(C), Montreal Dermatologic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting Cost versus quality

One should consider the following facts while choosing the coolsculpting cost.

  1. Does the price include a coolsculpt consultation with a qualified, experienced and board certified physician? Can your physician get a detailed health history and examine you personally before recommending the total number of treatments or alternatives?
  2. How long has the facility performed coolsculpting? Does the facility use the newer applicators?
  3. Do they offer Dualsculpting and other complimentary procedures such as Z wave treatment to enhance the coolsculpt results and reduce the post procedure discomfort?
  4. Is the facility invested in meeting your long term aestheic goals or is it offering deep discounts on a single procedure?
  5. Is the facility similar to an assembly line or will you receive personalized care? We were the first physician practice to offer Coolsculpting to the Northshore area of Boston,MA.

We have two machines and new applicators, I have personally personally performed over a thousand procedures. We are certified Coolsculpt practice and graduates of coolsculpting University. All of the above factors are included in our pricing. (Shanthala Shivananjappa, MD, Boston Physician)

Treatment cost varies from patient to patient

Coolsculpting cost range depending on which area you are needing fat reduction in . Also larger applicators generally cost more per cycle than the smaller applicators.

It would also depend on if you would need more than one treatment in the desired area as Coolsculpting affects 20-25% of the fat cells in treated area.

Some practices will discount the second treatment if its needed. It’s best to schedule a consultation to see exactly which applicators would work best for you and how many treatments it would take to achieve your desired results. (Pushpendra Jain, MD, Cookeville Physician)

Cost of Coolsculpting is dependent on the patient

Coolsculpting pricing is carefully monitored and set by Zeltiq so that pricing does not vary widely. It also depends on what the individual patient desires. Coolsculpting is a non-surgical option to surgery (liposuction) that results in 25% reduction of fat in the area.

It, like liposuction, is not a weight loss procedure. Similar results in certain areas can occur with Coolsculpting with repeated treatments. Most patients choose Coolsculpting over liposuction because Coolscupting does not require any anesthesia, requires no needles, and has no downtime.

As long as patients understand that Coolsculpting will not result in large volume fat reduction, it can be a great alternative to surgery. (Mark E. Mason, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)
