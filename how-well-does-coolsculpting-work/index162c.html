CoolSculpting Does Work

CoolSculpting is FDA-cleared for stubborn pockets of fat on the abdomen, flanks, inner/outer thighs, double-chin and upper arms that are not responsive to diet and exercise. Although liposuction is an effective option for removal of unwanted fat, CoolSculpting is an alternative that involves no surgery or recovery time and is focused on smaller fatty areas.

CoolSculpting uses innovative cooling technology developed at Harvard University to freeze fat cells without harming the surrounding skin. Your body then gradually eliminates the frozen fat cells. There have been more than 5 million CoolSculpting treatments performed worldwide for nonsurgical fat reduction.

CoolSculpting: It’s Not for Everyone

CoolSculpting is not nearly as effective as the groundbreaking treatment, SculpSure.

Using its advanced light-based laser technology, SculpSure is a non-invasive body contouring system that can eliminate up to 24% of stubborn fat in popular areas of the body such as the upper and lower abdomen, back, flanks, thighs and under the chin.

SculpSure is ideal for all skin and body types and upon consultation with your provider, treatment plans are customized to your aesthetic needs.

While CoolSculpting treatments may take 60-90 minutes per treatment, each SculpSure treatment only takes 25 minutes and there is no downtime! CoolSculpting can also be extremely painful and leave you with prolonged numbness, while SculpSure uses heat technology for a painless and relaxing experience.

With SculpSure, most patients see maximum results following a series of treatments. Be sure to ask your SculpSure provider about which treatment plan is right for you. (Kian Karimi, MD, FACS, Los Angeles Facial Plastic Surgeon)

For the right candidate, CoolSculpting is a very effective treatment for fat reduction in stubborn areas such as the abdomen, hips, thighs, arms, back fat, bra fat, under the chin, etc. The treatment uses controlled cooling to kill fat cells in the targeted area.

Over a period of weeks, the body eliminates the fat cells forever. The average patient can expect to see a 20-25% reduction of fat in the treated area. Results can be seen as early as three weeks, but can take up to 3 months for the full result to be visible.

Additional treatments can be performed if necessary to achieve the desired result. It is best to wait a period of 3 months after your treatment to determine if another session is needed. (Shamim Shakibai, MD, Los Angeles Physician)

Coolsculpting

Yes! Coolsculpting does work, but not on all types of fat. The fat that is amenable to treatment with Coolsculpting is called subcutaneous fat. This is fat directly beneath the skin. Visceral fat, or the fat that is deeper, and surrounds the organs, is not able to be treated with Coolsculpting.

The ideal type of fat for treatment with Coolsculpting has to be pinchable, and essentially easy to grab and take hold of with the Coolsculpting applicator. Your final result with a Coolsculpting technique won’t appear for approximately three months after treatment.

That being said, there is some evidence to suggest that the benefits of Coolsculpting treatment can be accentuated if your second treatment to the area occurs about a month after the first. (Mariam Cappiello, MD, Chestnut Hill Anesthesiologist)
