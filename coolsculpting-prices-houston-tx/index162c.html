How does cryolipolysis work?

So, cryolipolysis is non-invasive, a non-surgical technique for eliminating excess fat deposits. The cryolipolysis procedure is completely safe. To protect the skin from hypothermia, a special gasket is placed between the vacuum applicator and the body.

It also ensures an even distribution of the cold. Thus, cooling is ensured throughout the thickness of the fat fold, and hence, effective cleavage of fats and their subsequent processing in the course of metabolism. During the procedure, the body directs the defenses to heat the chilled area, and the fat cells begin to split violently.

The surrounding tissues – nerves, vessels, muscles, skin at the same time do not suffer at all. Important: with cryolipolysis, the removal of the products of the decomposition of fat cells does not give any load to the liver and the excretory system, since fat destruction occurs gradually, and not simultaneously, shockly, as with many other apparatus methods of destruction of local fatty deposits. When to wait for the results?

In contrast to the traumatic methods of getting rid of fat deposits, a quick result in cryolipolysis should not be expected. After all, the splitting of fats in this case is absolutely physiological, natural, and therefore everything will not happen immediately.

For one procedure, the number of fat cells decreases by 20-30%. The very process of decay of fats will occur at least 60-90 days. Is not it better to wait for a while, not experiencing any unpleasant sensations, than to suffer after the operation of liposuction?

Depending on your individual characteristics and wishes, you will need one or three procedures with an interval of approximately 1.5 months. You can be sure that you will get the first results within a month. Recommended by the developers of the method (and confirmed in practice) the course of cryolipolysis consists of 3 procedures with an interval of 1 month. When the full course is completed, deep fat burning mechanisms are started and the fat cells are reduced within 9 months after the end of the course.

Contraindications to the procedure:

  • cold allergy;
  • all acute conditions (angina, acute respiratory infections, herpetic eruptions, etc.)
  • presence of skin lesions in the area of the apparatus (scars, wounds, skin diseases in acute stage);
  • diseases of the circulatory system;
  • insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus;
  • the presence in the body of an artificial pacemaker driver, a pacemaker;
  • thrombosis;
  • Varicose veins 3 and 4 stages;
  • pregnancy and lactation.

How to save the effect? In many ways, the maintenance of the result of the procedure depends on you. If after the completion of the course of procedures you will: lead a healthy lifestyle, eat right, give yourself moderate exercise (walking, dancing, morning exercises, cycling) – you keep the effect of cryolipolysis stably.
