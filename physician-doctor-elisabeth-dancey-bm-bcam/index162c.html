What’s the best treatment to remove brown, age spots?
brown spots on face
Feb 27, 2020
Since you are having peeling you might want to ask your therapist to use a slightly stronger acid on these spots, for example TCA. This will reach deeper down in the skin and cause a stronger peel on these areas. They will go really dark brown and will peel.
Otherwise some simple cautery with the hyfrecator will achieve results with a similar downtime, around five days.
I’m 37 and I have severe Seborrheic Keratosis. Are there any medications, creams or surgery
Seborrheoic keratoses
Feb 27, 2020
These can be caused by sun exposure or sudden stress, often when your immune system is a bit down. Normally we remove them with electrocautery (the hyfrecator). This doesn’t hurt; we use ice or local anaesthetic as a numbing agent. I would also consider looking into other factors, such as your general health, nutrient intake, healthy fats intake, alcohol intake and stress levels. There are some interesting homeopathic agents that work specifically on lumps and bumps – so don’t give up.
What is the best way to remove an extremely dark possibly irregular age spot close to my eye
age spots close to eye
Feb 27, 2020
Any new spots or lesions on the face should be checked out first before removing them. Yes, once they have been declared to be simple age spots they can be removed by several non-invasive methods such as laser, cautery or peeling. Check with a qualified doctor especially when you have pigmented skin as there is a risk of hyper or hypo pigmentation. Surgery should be the last resort.

Age spots appear on the top part of the skin. Laser or any other treatment will be fine. Make sure that the spot is checked first to exclude anything more serious. Cautery with a gentle electrical heating current is easy, non-painful and precise. Normally one treatment is sufficient.

I’m getting filler in my cleft chin. Which one has produced the best results?
FILLER FOR CLEFT CHIN
Feb 20, 2020
Any filler with a high G prime (viscous) would work. Sometimes that little cleft has an attachment at the base making the job a bit harder for the injector and resulting in a less than expected result. The injector should be able to break down any tissue attachments with the needle or cannula that they are using. This might result in a bit of bruising and a bit more swelling but the results will be better and longer lasting.
