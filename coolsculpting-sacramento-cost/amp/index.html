What is Coolsculpting?

The beneficial effects of cold on the cells of the body have been known for a long time. Remember the use of ice cubes for facial massage to keep it in a tone and excellent condition. The assumption that fat is “afraid” of the cold, was confirmed. Proof of this is also a skin reaction to the cold – it becomes thinne

Lose weight by the cold is no longer a novelty, many will say. Cryosauna is common. That’s right. But the effect of cryosauna is only 5% of the possible. Conclusions make yourself. The fatty cage needs to be subjected to strong cooling so that it collapses.

In cryosauna, fat folds are cooled only superficially, and from the inside they are actively supplied with blood circulation, so the effect of dissolving fats slows down.

How does Cryolipolysis work?

Consider the order of the procedure. First of all, the patient is examined, and then, on those parts of his body where coolsculpting is planned to be applied, the master applies gel-antifreeze. The vacuum nozzle sucks in adipose tissue and activates the process of its processing with cold throughout its volume. The procedure takes from 40 minutes to an hour and does not cause discomfort.

You can take a comfortable position, read, watch a movie, drink tea and even sleep. At the end of the session, you can return to your normal life.

What are the indications to Coolsculpting?

The causes are different, and the most common are fat deposits on the abdomen, waist, sides, buttocks, back, inner thigh, over the knees, at the top of the hands.

The procedure of cryolipolysis is used by people who intend to participate in competitions (the task at the same time is losing weight and placing emphasis on the relief of muscles).

In addition, cryolipolysis is an excellent alternative in the case when there are contraindications to other methods of correction of the body.

Contraindications

  • violation of blood circulation in the treatment zone,
  • take of anticoagulants,
  • hypersensitivity to cold (cold urticaria),
  • impaired skin sensitivity,
  • fresh injuries, wounds, scars and other skin lesions in the treatment area,
  • hernia,
  • diabetes,
  • neuropathic disorders,
  • pacemaker,
  • pregnancy and the period of breastfeeding,
  • fat folds less than 2.5 cm thick.

How many procedures will it take?

To achieve a pronounced and satisfactory result, 1 procedure is sufficient. The course can be from 3 to 5 procedures individually, according to the indications.

How often can cryolipolysis be done?

After 21-28 days, the procedure can be repeated locally to enhance the effect in problem areas.
