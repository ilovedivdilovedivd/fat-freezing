Coolsculpting Male Breast Before & After

Coolsculpting for enlarged male breasts

Male breast enlargement is often a combination of both extra fat and extra breast tissue (referred to as gynecomastia), and sometimes extra skin as well (as in the case of patients who have had massive weight loss).

Techniques such as Coolsculpting or liposuction will address any fat that’s causing the breast enlargement, but they won’t address gynecomastia tissue or excess skin.

Depending upon a patients anatomy, Coolsculpting may be a reasonable option, although an evaluation by a plastic surgeon knowledgeable about surgical management of gynecomastia or excess skin will be best to understand all the potential options.

In order to get a qualified, ethical, and expert opinion on your surgical options and expectations, schedule an in-person office consultation with a plastic surgeon certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery. (Keith M. Blechman, MD, New York Plastic Surgeon)

CoolSculpting For Man Boobs

The male chest area (man boobs) can be successfully treatment with CoolSculpting as long as the enlargement is due to fat and not glandular tissue.

You would need an in-person consultation and evaluation to determine if the treatment is appropriate for you.

Most men need between 2-3 treatments for optimal results. Make sure you see an experienced provider. (Michael A. Zadeh, MD, FACS, Sherman Oaks General Surgeon)

It can be used for gynecomastia. There maybe a need for a second treatment , you should first be examined to determine if you are an adequate candidate by someone who also performs surgical improvement for this area. (Drew Varano, MD, Washington DC Physician)

The male enlarged breast will usually respond well to CoolSculpting. A technique utilizing overlap is commonly performed. Sometimes a precautionary mammogram is obtained. (Jeffrey Rapaport, MD, Englewood Dermatologic Surgeon)

This area has been treated successfully in our practice. You should be seen and examined by a Plastic Surgeon to know all of your options. (Robert Whitfield, MD, FACS, Austin Plastic Surgeon)
