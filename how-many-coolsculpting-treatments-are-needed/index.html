How Many Coolsculpting Treatments Are Needed?

It is impossible to give you a specific answer because each patient will have individual needs. It is important that you have a consultation so that your target areas can be seen and examined.

Your expectations and goals are very important in determining what will be best for you. Best results occur when each target area is treated at least twice with the two treatments being done about one month apart.

Coolsculpting treatments and getting the best results

I would advised seeing a physician’s office where the doctor is involved in the process. (Omar Ibrahimi, MD, PhD, Stamford Dermatologic Surgeon)

Time interval

The time interval between treatments varies depending on your goals. You can approach it two ways. If you know you will need at least 2 treatments to get the results you want then doing them a month apart makes sense because you will improve your results with another 20-25% of fat cells killed.

You have the benefit of seeing results sooner than waiting the 3-4 months and then doing a second treatment. The coolsculpting will not affect the body’s ability to remove the previously killed fat cells. If you only have a small amount of fatty tissue in an area and you dont know if you will need a second treatment then you can wait until you see your full results, about 4 month, and decide. (Michelle J. Place, MD, Danville Plastic Surgeon)

Swelling after liposuction

Liposuction leaves the tissue on the inside under the skin swollen and irritated. It also disrupts the drainage to the treated areas temporarily and this makes the areas feel more firm, swollen, and sometimes lumpy.

Most of the swelling is gone in the first 1-2 months, but it can take up to six months for all of the inflammation and swelling to go away. Massage can help the process go faster for you.

For best results, you should wait at least 6 months for all of the swelling to go away before having any more treatments. (Dana Coberly, MD, Tampa Plastic Surgeon)

It would be very important to recover from your other procedure before being treated but you may benefit from treatment with the flap CoolSculpting applicator. (Robert Whitfield, MD, FACS, Austin Plastic Surgeon)
