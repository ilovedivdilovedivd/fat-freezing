Does Coolsculpting Work On Saddlebags?

Applicator Now Available for Saddlebags Treatment

CoolSculpting has just introduced the CoolSmooth applicator designed to treat outer thighs. This applicator does not rely on suction to deliver cooling as the other applicators do and consequently can treat “non-pinchable” bulges.

The treatment takes 2 hours per area (instead of the typical 1 hour with the other applicators) so the total treatment time for both saddlebags would be 4 hours. (Steven Goldman, MD, Cleveland Plastic Surgeon)

The Cool Smooth Applicator is for the outer thighs

This new applicator has just been FDA approved and will be shipped out to practice shortly. It was designed to treat the lateral thighs!

Give us a call in Austin to find out when it is available. (Robert Whitfield, MD, FACS, Austin Plastic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting Saddlebags Fat Reduction

CoolSculpting on Tough to treat areas

The outer thigh is a tough area to treat with CoolSculpting because the fat in that area is usually visceral and fibrous, and this type of fat does not draw into the applicator well. We have seen good results on inner thighs with the CoolFit applicator.

Your doctor should be able to tell if your saddlebags can be treated. If you can draw into the applicator it should work. If you don’t have a good draw, frankly don’t waste your money.

CoolSculpting is a great technology and it offers fantastic results, but if you don’t have good contact with the cooling plates it will not work. (Sarah A. Mess, MD, Columbia Plastic Surgeon)

CoolSculpting for Saddlebags

Coolsculpting Saddlebags Fat

There is a small applicator that will work on areas such as the saddlebags. However, there has to be enough volume (fat tissue) to be able to maintain the suction of the applicator.

Sometimes that area of the body just doesn’t have a large enough fat pocket to work with this device.

That’s not to say it won’t work, but that’s where you would need to have your provider view the area you want treated and give you a recommendation. (Jeffrey W. Hall, MD, Austin Plastic Surgeon)

Zeltiq (CoolSculpting) to Treat Fat On Upper Outer Thighs?

A new applicator is available to treat the thighs — check with your provider to see if he or she is currently using it. (Michael Gold, MD, Nashville Dermatologic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting on the outer thigh

Until 2014, Coolsculpting was designed for the trunk and not for the thigh. The Zeltig corporation has now released a new Coolsculpting head specifically designed for use on the outer thigh. Although it is new, the early results are promising. (John Zavell, MD, FACS, Toledo Plastic Surgeon)

 

 

 

Upper outer thighs

Zeltiq Coolsculpting Treatment For Saddlebags

Coolsculpting has been given FDA clearance for thighs this year. If the patient is a good candidate, then the results can be achieved. (Kris M. Reddy, MD, FACS, West Palm Beach Plastic Surgeon)

CoolSculpting for Outer Thighs

There is now a CoolSculpting applicator formulated for the lateral thighs. It provides an alternative to liposuction for those who do not wish to have surgery. Go for an in person consultation to be examined. (Francisco Canales, MD, Santa Rosa Plastic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting Thighs

Zeltiq has a new flat hand piece that is specifically designed for areas such as the upper out thighs where it would be hard to get a vacuum seal that the other applicators require. Each treatment should result in a 15-25% reduction of fat.

FDA clearance for thigh treatment was received in April of 2014. (Sanjiv Kayastha, MD, Albany Plastic Surgeon)

Can Coolsculpting be used on the outer thighs?

Currently, Coolsculpting is not recommended for the saddlebags because the tissues in this area are stiffer and can not be drawn into the handpiece enough to allow for a successful treatment. (Janet Woodyard, MD, Baltimore Plastic Surgeon)
