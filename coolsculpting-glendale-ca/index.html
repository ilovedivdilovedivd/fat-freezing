Coolsculpting In Glendale CA

Coolsculpting is a modern non-surgical technique of the body correction, which allows you to get rid of fat deposits with the help of cold.

This procedure is applicable to any problem zone on the body: abdomen, buttocks, waist, hips.

Women, who have tried the innovative method on themselves, speak of it as an effective and painless method, allowing for a short time to get rid of excess volumes. And as a proof, they place photos that allow you to evaluate the figure before and after cryolipolysis.

The procedure, unlike many methods aimed at losing weight, is not associated with painful injections, because it is based on the use of low temperatures.

The cold effect on certain areas of the body is pleasant to many, it causes pleasant sensations and at the same time it works to destroy adipose tissue.

We will get acquainted with the unique procedure and learn about its advantages, disadvantages and peculiarities of carrying out.

Coolsculpting: how does it work?

Coolsculpting is a simple, invasive procedure, based on long (1 hour) cooling of the fat layer in problem areas. The fat cells of our body are very sensitive to low temperatures, under the influence of which they are destroyed naturally.

Such death is not dangerous for the body, as surrounding tissues do not suffer, inflammatory reactions do not occur and the products of the decomposition of fats do not contaminate the blood and lymphatic system.

Thus, the cryolipolysis procedure is a safe alternative to liposuction is a method in which fat is sucked off by puncturing the skin on the body. An innovative procedure was developed by a group of American scientists who proved that the prolonged cooling of fat tissue at a certain temperature begins the process of apoptosis (natural destruction). This discovery allowed to create an apparatus that provided the necessary cooling of the problem areas of the body and caused the destruction of fat cells.

Indications for the procedure are:

  • Obesity of the alimentary-constitutional type, inherent in people leading a sedentary lifestyle.
  • Obesity of the hypothalamic type. Weight gain in this case occurs as a result of damage to one of the brain areas (hypothalamus), which provokes abnormal eating behavior and excessive consumption of high-calorie food.
  • Obesity, which develops as a result of endocrine pathologies. In this category of patients, metabolism is disturbed, which leads to weight gain even with low-calorie foods.
  • Obesity, provoked by mental illness. In this case, a violation of the food balance causes certain medications.

At the same time, there are a number of contraindications to the procedure.

Coolsculpting Is A New Fat Removal System That Works By Freezing Cells That Effectively Destroys Them

Coolsculpting can not be done under the following conditions:

  • Hypersensitivity to cold (cold allergy)
  • A pacemaker
  • Neurological disorders of various etiologies;
  • Mechanical damage to the skin in the places of expected treatment
  • Presence of skin diseases (eczema, dermatitis)
  • The period of pregnancy and breastfeeding
  • Diabetes
  • Poor blood clotting

Procedures can be done at any time of the year, regardless of solar activity. The number of procedures a patient needs is determined by a specialist. On average, the loss of body fat after the course of treatment is up to 35%, which is considered a very good indicator.

Resilience of result

The effect after the procedure is fairly persistent. With proper nutrition and exercise, the result can be kept for many years. In the case of a passive lifestyle and abuse of harmful, high-calorie food, the lost volume will return within a year, if not repeat the procedure systematically.

Side effects and complications

The manifestations of side effects after coolsculpting treatment as a whole are minimal. The most common symptoms are:

  • Unpleasant sensations during the procedure (itching, pain, burning). It is usually seen in the first few minutes after the start of exposure.
  • Numbness of affected areas, loss of sensitivity.
  • Redness of the skin, the appearance of bruises.
  • All these negative manifestations usually take place within 1-2 weeks after the procedure.

More troubling symptoms are:

  • convulsions;
  • circulatory disorders;
  • frostbite;
  • the appearance of blisters at the treatment site.

If you find these symptoms, you should immediately consult a doctor. Most often they indicate about the use of non-certified equipment.

Contraindications

There are also contraindications to the procedure. In particular, cryolipolysis should not be performed in people who have:

  • Raynaud’s disease (lesion of small terminal arteries and arterioles);
  • excessive sensitivity to low temperatures;
  • period of pregnancy and lactation;
  • presence of a pacemaker;
  • problem skin in the areas of the procedure;
  • recently transferred operations;
  • scars on the treated areas;
  • mental illness;
  • kidney pathologies;
  • diabetes.

Is the cryolipolysis procedure possible at home?

At the moment, there are several mobile devices for carrying out cryolipolysis. But doctors do not recommend using them, since most of them have not been certified and are fake. When using such devices it is impossible to achieve the same effect as in the clinic, on the contrary, it can lead to frostbite and damage to the skin.
