Cryolipolysis is the procedure for removing local fat deposits as a result of the effect of low temperature on problem areas. The impact occurs with the help of a special apparatus without damaging the skin. Cryolipolysis was invented by the staff of the hospital at the Harvard University School of Medicine Dr. R. Rox Anderson and Dr. D. Manstein.

The principle of cryolipolysis

The procedure of Coolsculpting is absolutely painless and safe for the organism. This device creates a slight vibration during the procedure, in contrast to the previous generation devices that use vacuum attachments, which can result in painful sensations, as well as the appearance of swelling, bruising and redness of the skin.

The device allows you to work on four problem areas simultaneously. Subcutaneous fatty tissue cools, resulting in fat cells killing and gradually reducing the fat layer within 2 to 4 months after the procedure.

The dead fat cells are then transported by the body for processing into the liver, like fats, which we take with food. Thus, there is a gradual reduction in fat tissue without side effects and the formation of toxic decomposition products.

Cryolipolysis excludes exposure to other organs and systems of the body and does not require additional procedures. This procedure is an alternative to plastic surgery and injection.

Results from the application of cryolipolysis

Coolsculpting can achieve lasting results due to the fact that it acts directly on adipose tissue. Currently, it is the most effective, fastest and safest way to get rid of excess local fat deposits compared to other methods.

Safety of CoolSculpting

The safety and effectiveness of the cryolipolysis procedure was confirmed by the results of scientific research. The procedure is internationally certified and approved by the FDA (Food and Drug Administration in the United States) in the De Novo category.

Indications for the use of Coolsculpting treatment

If you have visible fat folds from 2.5 cm to 6 cm in the abdomen, back, sides or knees area and you are looking for a procedure that can relieve you of unwanted body fat and does not bring pain and side effects, then cryolipolysis is your choice.

Contraindications for CoolSculpting

Coolsculpting Targets And Cools Unwanted Fat Cells In The Selected Area To Induce A Noticeable, Natural-looking Reduction In Fat Bulges

Nevertheless, for cryolipolysis, there are a number of contraindications:

  • high sensitivity to low temperatures
  • excessive obesity
  • circulatory disturbance in the problem area
  • Pregnancy and lactation
  • diabetic polyneuropathy
  • presence of pacemaker
  • postherpetic neuralgia
  • blood clotting disorder
  • open wounds or scars in the problem area
  • a hernia in the problem area.

The price and duration of the coolsculpting procedure

Usually the coolsculpting lasts from 1 to 3 hours. You can comfortably sit in a sitting or lying position and spend this time with benefit: for reading or work. The procedure is carried out only by appointment.

Are there any factors to improve the result Cryolipolysis?

Of course, the right way of life enhances the effect of the procedure. But in order to get a pronounced aesthetic result, we recommend, in one program, to combine cryolipolysis with vibrational training on PowerPlate.

This activates the muscles and the process of thermogenesis, accelerates the processes of metabolism and evacuation of fat. In particularly resistant areas, such as fat deposits on the back, we combine cryolyspolysis with radio-wave action and / or injection lipolysis.

The addition of certain amino acids (Gunamino Formula) in a daily diet in combination with certain vitamins, for example Vit Formula, helps positively affect BMI.

What additional procedures are recommended to be combined with CoolSculpting?

As shown by the studios on 50 volunteers that were conducted at the University of Naples by Dr. Ferraro, a combination of cryolipolysis with shock wave therapy enhanced the remodeling of collagen fibers of the skin, because in itself cryolipolysis has no effect aimed at tightening the skin. Lymphatic drainage accelerates the resolution of edema.

Is CoolSculpting equally effective for all patients?

Based on our experience, 5-7% of patients are resistant to this procedure. This is due to many reasons, such as ethnic, sexual and hormonal. If you suddenly fall into these 5-7%, in our clinic we are conducting a second session in combination with radio wave lipolysis.

Are there any age limitations for coolsculpting therapy?

This procedure is quite effective for all patients of different skin types and age groups. However, in our clinic, we do not apply this technique to patients under 18 years of age.
