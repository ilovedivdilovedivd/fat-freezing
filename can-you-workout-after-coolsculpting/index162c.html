Coolsculpting vs Liposuction

Coolsculpting and liposuction are both great options for fat removal. Coolsculpting is ideal for someone who works out regularly and eats healthy but just has some stubborn areas of fat on their body. The procedure is non-invasive, which means there are no needles and it can be done it just a few hours at an office setting.

Liposuction is much more invasive and usually requires the patient to be under general anesthesia. The results are much more dramatic and will be seen much faster than the results from coolsculpting. In terms of downtime after Coolsculpting, you can expect a few days of soreness and some minor bruising and swelling.

You can start working out again the next day if you‚Äôd like. Regarding your liposuction recovery, you will most likely be looking at 1-2 weeks of downtime and you want to wait at least a few weeks to a month before you start working out again. (Jeffrey Rockmore, MD, Albany Plastic Surgeon)

Some swelling and soreness in the area treated is expected and normal post Cool/Sculpting. The more areas treated the more swelling and soreness expected. Because CoolSculpting is non-invasive there are no restrictions to amount or type of exercise after the procedure.
