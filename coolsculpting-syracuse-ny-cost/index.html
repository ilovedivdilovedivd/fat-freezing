Cryolipolysis is the procedure for effective control of local fat deposits. It is based on selective and controlled cooling of adipose tissue with subsequent triggering of death of adipocytes.

The method of cryolipolysis differs from many traditional methods, both hardware and injection, as it excludes fibroplasia, trauma to the skin surface or scarring. At the same time, during a gradual reduction in the volume of fatty tissues, there is no pressure on internal organs or systemic effects on the body. The method is based on prolonged hypoxia, as well as reducing the temperature of subcutaneous fat to +25 degrees, which, as a result, leads to the following physiological process: the natural death of fat cells (apoptosis). The duration of this process is 50-70 days.

The newest technology of cryolipolysis excludes cold damage to the nerves, skin, vessels or internal organs, because the freezing temperature of triglycerides is much higher than the freezing temperature of other biological substances and liquids. During the procedure, the vacuum applicator is installed on the chosen problem zone. Between the vacuum applicator and the skin, a napkin impregnated with a special solution is placed, which protects the skin from possible supercooling. With the help of vacuum, the fat fold is captured inside this applicator and cooled for one hour.

Equipment for cryolipolysis

The first device for the cryolipolysis procedure was CoolSculpting from Zeltiq Aesthetics (USA). Despite the fact that there are already analogues of the original equipment mainly of Chinese production, it is widely used around the world in many beauty clinics and beauty salons, is considered the most safe and popular. In addition, the technology implemented in the device Coolsculpting (Zeltiq Aesthetics), is protected by a number of patents, so “copying” it by other manufacturers is illegal.

CoolSculpting Is Quick, Easy And Effective

Apparatus for cryolipolysis is equipped with a special applicator, inside of which, under the influence of vacuum, the fat fold is captured, and then cooled from both sides. In the process of sucking, fat is separated from the underlying muscles or internal organs, which prevents cold from affecting them.

The nozzles used in the device have different sizes, shapes and features for working with certain areas of the body. To protect the skin from frostbite on their inner side there is a protective coating. The kit for CoolSculpting includes applicators for the following zones:

  • small folds on the bends of the body;
  • large folds;
  • small and medium folds on the sides and abdomen;
  • folds in hard-to-reach places;
  • small longitudinal folds on the inner surface of the thigh, abdomen and back.

A special nozzle has also been developed, the design of which provides a very tight fit to the body and allows the production of cryolipolysis without the use of vacuum, which reduces the likelihood of bruising and hematomas.

This method is based on the natural process of the body. With all the rules, fat is split, without harming the skin, blood vessels, muscles. The procedure does not give an instantaneous result, and, therefore, the body has time to withdraw the products of the fatty layer disintegration without excessive load on the liver and kidneys.

The coolsculpting treatment is carried out without anesthesia, and, therefore, the human body is not overloaded with unnecessary pharmacological drugs.

Coolsculpting refers to non-invasive methods of combating fat deposits.

As a result, this is the absence of scars and a recovery period. In time and after the session of cryolipolysis, a person does not experience painful sensations. Only a small discomfort is possible. The method of treatment makes it possible to reduce the volume in one particular zone.

Cryolipolysis among many advantages is characterized by a number of disadvantages. They include the following facts:

The procedure has a fairly high cost. Cryolipolysis does not give a quick result. Failure to comply with the correct diet and lack of small physical exertion is a possibility of deposition of new fat cells in the treated area.

The procedure has a number of side effects.

Indications and contraindications to the procedure

Coolsculpting treatment can be assigned to a person in the following situations:

In the event that a person has alimentary-constitutional obesity. This type of obesity is inherent in people who are inactive and are prone to constant overeating.
In the case of disruption of the endocrine system of the body and the manifestation of endocrinological obesity.

Obesity associated with a malfunction of the digestive system, the cause of which is the disruption of the hypothalamus.

The presence of obesity for psychological reasons. In the case when due to a disorder of the mental system, a person is prone to overeating.

Carrying out the cryolipolysis procedure is permissible in a number of the above cases of obesity.

The prohibition or restriction on the coolsculpting session can be justified in the following situations:

Raynaud’s syndrome is considered one of the main contraindications to the procedure.

The presence of an electrocardiostimulator in humans does not allow for the procedure.

The increased sensitivity of the body to low temperatures can become a contraindication to carrying out cryolipolysis.

Women during pregnancy and during lactation.

In the case of recently undergone surgical operations with cryogenic liposuction is necessary to wait until the body is restored and the scars will heal.

If the place of the fat fold has open wounds or unhealed scars, the cryolipolysis procedure is not performed until the wound is healed. In cases of pathological obesity, the procedure is not carried out.
