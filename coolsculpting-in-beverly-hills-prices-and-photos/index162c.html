Coolsculpting is a new effective non-surgical technique for removing adipose tissue by cooling. It allows you to significantly reduce body volume and the appearance of cellulite without a long period of rehabilitation.

The effectiveness of the methodology is confirmed by one of the most authoritative and rigorous medical certification organizations.

Cryolipolysis is a gentle effect and powerful effect!

Features of cryolipolysis

In the process of cryoliposuction, cold exposure to a specific area of the body occurs, where there are fat folds, cellulite, local fat deposits in women and men!

How does it work? The procedure is carried out using a special apparatus. Its vacuum nozzle “captures” the fat fold and cools it to the optimum temperature, which acts solely on the fat cells, without damaging surrounding tissues.

As a result of such a temporary “freezing”, fat cells begin to decrease in size and are gradually eliminated from the body naturally.

In one cryolipolysis procedure, the volume of adipose tissue in the affected area can be reduced by 25%. For 2 procedures – by 40-50%. Due to a gradual decrease in fat, the skin in the problem area does not hang.

The procedure of cryolipolysis does not require anesthesia and surgical intervention, does not load the liver and other internal organs. Immediately after the procedure you can return to normal business!

What does cryolipolysis give you?

  • Effective fight against cellulite and puffiness
  • Increase the elasticity and elasticity of the skin

Results

The first results of coolsculpting can be seen 2-3 weeks after the procedure. The full course usually consists of 1-4 procedures with breaks of 21-28 days.

The maximum effect will appear after 1.5-2 months: this is how long the process of gradually eliminating the body from the products of the decomposition of fat cells will take. We offer our permanent and new customers a guaranteed result!

Contraindications

  • Cold urticaria and other acute reactions to exposure to low temperatures;
  • Circulatory disorders in the problem area;
  • Diabetes;
  • Postherpetic neuralgia;
  • Violation of blood clotting;
  • Open wounds, scars, hernia in the affected area;
  • Pregnancy and lactation.

Indications to Coolsculpting

  • excessive fat deposits in the abdomen and waist;
  • fatty folds in the region of the lateral or posterior surfaces of the back;
  • “Breeches” and excess fat on the inner surface of the thigh;
  • fat deposits in the knees;
  • cellulite and flabby skin on the inner surface of the hands.
