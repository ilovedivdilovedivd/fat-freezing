Cryolipolysis is the splitting of body fat by cooling. This procedure can be considered the best means of liposuction without surgery. This method does not require anesthesia.

It is impossible to damage the skin. Moreover, in a moment after the procedure you can safely return to your usual affairs. In the period of getting rid of extra pounds, fatty elements (adipocytes) are cooled. You do not need to resort to surgery.

The device freezes only adipocytes and does not touch adjacent tissues and nerves due to a fixed applicator. Non-surgical liposuction continues because adipocytes begin to die under the influence of cold. These components are then transferred through the lymphatic system for processing, like fats, obtained by a person with food.

Coolsculpting: the principle of action

This method is patented by famous scientists working in the Massachusetts hospital, as well as at Harvard University. During the procedure, the cooling reaches -5 degrees Celsius.

This damages those cells that in the body form adipose tissue and are called adipocytes, which leads to a reduction in the unnecessary layer under the skin. Then the dead elements simply leave the human body, and do so without harm to his health.

This technique is called the newest method of cooling, which is necessarily controlled. It does not destroy the integrity of the cell physically. With this method, nerves, vessels and internal organs remain unaffected.

Its purpose is to affect only the fat components. The process itself lasts from 2 to 3 months. As a result, the number of adipocytes decreases, which is equal to 20-30% after even one procedure.

Preparing for coolsculpting

Coolsculpting does not require any special preparation. It can be carried out at any time of the day. The procedure does not carry a preliminary restriction on physical activity and food intake. Preparation for the procedure can be considered a visit to a doctor who will determine whether there are any contraindications to the procedure.

Stages of cryolipolysis

One session lasts about one astronomical hour. The procedure consists of several stages:

The next step is to apply the gel to the marked area. This gel is a conductor.

After applying a special gel, one of the attachments of the cryolipolysis apparatus is applied to the marked fold. It captures the crease and creates a vacuum space.

Cold exposure to fatty deposits in this area begins. It lasts 60 minutes. During this period, fat cells start energy starvation, and they disintegrate. Decay products are eliminated from the body gradually.

The first result is due to this in a few weeks. In order that there is no disturbance of blood circulation in the cooled zone, the applicator of apparatus simultaneously cools the area and conducts vibro-massage. At the end of the coolsculpting treatment, the vacuum maniple is removed. Remains of gel from the skin are removed with a napkin.

Effect and results from the coolsculpting treatment

One cryolipolysis procedure is capable of removing 20-30% of body fat in the selected area. To achieve 100% of the result, it is recommended to hold 3-4 sessions.

This is considered one course. The course can be repeated no earlier than 4 months. One treatment is held once in 2 months.

The first reduction in volume can be seen after a few weeks or a month.

The process of disintegration of fatty deposits and their removal from the body of each passes individually. With this, a different period for obtaining the first results is associated.

The final effect of the procedure will be visible after 3-4 months from the last session.

Side effects and possible complications

The following side effects may occur after coolsculpting treatment:

  • There may be a slight numbness in the area treated with cold.
  • In the zone subjected to cryolipolysis, sensitivity may temporarily decrease.
  • Due to the tightening of the fat fold in the nozzle of the apparatus and the creation of a vacuum, reddening of the skin or the appearance of a small hematoma can be observed.
  • All side effects are local in nature. When they occur, treatment is not required. All the consequences take place within a few weeks.
  • If the procedure is performed incorrectly or the service is provided on a counterfeit device, various complications may occur, including frostbite and circulatory disorders in the treated area.

In the case of high sensitivity to cold complications can not be avoided. Carrying out the procedure in such a situation can lead to severe allergies or cold hemoglobinuria.
