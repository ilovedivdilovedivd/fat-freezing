– In my consultation, I asked about the pain and was told that only happens when you use the large device, not the small one. 11 days ago I had 3 sections coolsculpting done (lower abdomen and each side of my waist) with the small device. True to the brochure, the procedure was fairly painless. After an initial discomfort, the area was numb for the remaining 50 minutes before the 5-minute massage, which was painful. I thought the worst was over. Not So. I have a high-pain tolerance and have given birth to two large babies without an epidural, but the recovery from CoolSculpting has been the most painful experience of my life. Not only did I swell up the first 3 days so that I could not wear pants or underwear, but although numb on the outside, I felt like I had been beaten with a bat on the inside. I could not sleep or sit upright, and Tylenol 3 did not help. About Day 5, the “zaps” started which must be what the nerve pain with shingles feels like. Just when I thought I had really damaged myself, on Day 9, I started feeling more normal. Almost all the bloating is down and I can sleep, and it only hurts when I press on it. I haven’t seen one bit of slimming, and hope that I didn’t get completely suckered by spending all this morning to feel such pain without any result. I would not recommend this to anyone.
