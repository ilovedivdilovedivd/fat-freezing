Cryolipolysis: what is it?

The procedure of cryolipolysis is quite common in the USA. This trend was formed due to the existing propagation of a healthy lifestyle and care for the body. In general, cryolipolysis is the most innovative, to date, procedure for eliminating fat deposits and adjusting the body shape with a technical device.

The method consists in exposing the body cells to temperatures below zero degrees. They have a different degree of resistance to cold, which is due to different rates of blood saturation, intracellular metabolism and oxygen consumption.

For example, skin cells, easily tolerate cooling and rapidly recover, because the skin is adapted to contact with the external environment. In contrast, they subcutaneously subcutaneously fatty tissue, which are in the body for the purpose of accumulating nutrients.

This cellular structure is not adapted to changes in temperature and is destroyed by cold exposure. That is, fatty tissue when it is affected by low temperatures is damaged, cells die and gradually disintegrate.

According to reviews about cryolipolysis, the benefits of the procedure include:

  • the slow dying off of fat cells, which does not violate the general processes in the body;
  • atraumatism;
  • mineralization of side effects;
  • combination with other manipulations, sports activities and diets;
  • removal of layers of fatty tissue and toxic products by natural means through lymph and blood;
  • there are no characteristic cavities on the body due to fat redistribution at the site of the treatment;
  • uniform decrease in volume.

Device for cryolipolysis

The fat burning procedure is carried out with the help of a special devive for cryolipolysis. They are of two types:

  • with nozzle-vacuum;
  • with a thin flat surface of metal.

Device for cryolipolysis, in which vacuum is applied.

Device with a vacuum nozzle have a special nozzel in which the skin and subcutaneously fatty tissue is sucked up by the formation of a vacuum. Cooling parts approach the skin from different edges, reducing the temperature of the exposure zone to -5 degrees.

At the same time, the subcutaneous tissue that causes energy starvation of the cells, their destruction and, in the course of time, the elimination in a natural way, is affected by adipocytes.

It should be noted that the fat splitting after the cryolipolysis procedure is carried out gradually, approximately, during 3-5 weeks after coolsculpting.

Device for cryolipolysis with a surface of metal of flat type.

Device for coolsculpting with a flat surface of metal is based on the principle of efficient heat removal from a fatty tissue when the skin is cooled by contact. For this, heat-removing plates are used.

At the same time, the devices themselves have several software protection levels that control the cooling process and exclude the thermal injuries.

  • neck;

Cryolipolysis of the abdomen is very popular today. As noted by those who tried the procedure on themselves, this technique is most effective on the abdomen.

Among the indications for Cryolipolysis can be noted as follows:

alimentary-constitutional obesity. This type of obesity is progressing in people with unhealthy diet, a sedentary lifestyle, dislike for sports loads and overeating;
hypothalamic obesity.

This type of obesity occurs when there is damage to the hypothalamus (one of the brain systems), which disrupts the work of the center of the nervous system, which is responsible for eating.

This is a nutritional disorder. At the same time, people with food consumption, does not feel satiety, and continues to eat in excess, which leads to overeating and the accumulation of fat in the subcutaneous tissue;

obesity due to diseases of the endocrine system. People who are deficient in the work of endocrine glands are not uncommonly obese.

All this is due to the fact that the intensity of metabolism changes and the body does not have time to properly process food;

obesity due to mental illness. Diseases associated with mental disorders often cause uncontrolled eating behavior and are the cause of obesity.

It is worth noting that if obesity is alimentary-constitutional, coolsculpting is carried out as an individual procedure. In other cases, except coolsculpting treatment, drug treatment is required. How does cryolipolysis work?

Cryolipolysis is a fairly simple procedure. Cryolipolysis is recommended to be performed in specialized beauty salons or clinics.

In general, cryolipolysis takes place in several stages.

  • The specialist makes a measurement of fat deposits and, depending on their structure and volume, selects one of the four nozzles.
  • The gasket from the gel is put on the after-treatment zone.
  • The applicator draws a fold of fat and cools it.
  • The session lasts from 45 minutes to 1 hour.

During a hardware exposure, the patient may feel tingling or pressure at the beginning of the procedure. Pain during coolsculpting treatment is absent, although many people say that during the session, they felt discomfort and cold.

After removing the applicators, clients often complain of numbness in the area of exposure and a slight chill. In addition, in place of the procedure, there is redness or blueness. All side effects take place within 30 minutes after coolsculpting treatment.

Cryolipolysis: contraindications

Like all innovative procedures, cryolipolysis has a number of contraindications. They include:

  • Raynaud’s disease;
  • high body temperature;
  • chills;
  • obesity in the advanced stage;
  • paroxysmal cold hemoglobinuria;
  • cold urticaria;
  • pregnancy and lactation;
  • the presence of scars, wounds and skin diseases at the site of the intended impact;
  • pacemaker.
