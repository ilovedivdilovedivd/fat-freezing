Coolsculpting San Diego Prices And Photos

Cryolipolysis is the cryotherapy of fat removal.

The system has a selective and controlled cooling effect on fat cells (adipocytes), resulting in a significant and natural reduction in fat in the abdomen, waist, back, sides, buttocks and hands.

The procedure is atraumatic and painless.

How does the cryolipolysis apparatus work?

A special sensor is applied to the treated zone, combining the effect of cryoletrophoresis (used to stimulate the subcutaneous tissue) and cryotechnology (for selective cooling of fat cells).

Adipocytes contained in the subcutaneous tissue are cells saturated with fatty acids, which is why they are sensitive even to a slight decrease in temperature.

In contrast, the peripheral nervous system, blood vessels, melanocytes (pigment cells), as well as fibrocytes (cells of connective tissue) are less susceptible to cold. Thus, removal of adipocytes occurs without any damage to adjacent tissues.

How is the removal of fat cells?

In the process of reducing fat deposits, the adipocytes crystallize, followed by apoptosis (death) and complete decay of cells with a gradual release of fats. The whole process takes 2 to 3 weeks.

How many sessions of cool sculpting are needed to be effective?

Depending on the fat layer and the desired result, it takes from 1 to 3 sessions. Preferred time of interruption between coolsculpting sessions is about 20 days.

How soon will the results be visible?

Depending on the initial thickness of the adipose tissue, the average loss is 3 to 15 mm, which is equivalent to 25-45%. Approximately 40% of the result from the cryolipolysis procedures will be noticeable after 15 days, during the following days the effect will increase. The decision on subsequent procedures is taken within three weeks after the first coolsculpting session.

How long does the treatment last?

To achieve a significant reduction in body fat in the treated area, it may be necessary to use more than one device to achieve the desired result. For example, for the abdomen, two applications of the device are necessary, for the waist – 1 application on each side, for the lower back – 2 applications.

The duration of the effect of the device on one zone is 40-60 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fat folds. However, in some cases, for a full treatment of the treatment area, the duration of one session can be from 40 to 180 minutes.

The action is aimed at local fat deposits, so this procedure is not suitable for patients with general obesity. It is optimal for those who want to reduce body fat in specific areas, for example, in the abdomen, waist, lower back, hips, buttocks or hands.

What does the patient feel during and immediately after the Coolsculpting?

During the procedure, patients do not experience painful sensations. There is only a slight tension in the treated area due to the cryophoresis effect, and at the beginning of the procedure, a slight burning sensation from the cold is possible.

After the procedure, up to several hours, redness and numbness in the area of exposure may persist. Immediately after the cryolipolysis session, the patient can return to normal life without much discomfort.

What are the advantages of Coolsculpting before other procedures?

The system aimed at local and controlled cooling of adipocytes (fat cells) allows achieving significant and natural reduction of fatty deposits.

At the same time, the possibility of skin disorders is completely excluded, as in the case of invasive methods (surgical liposuction), there is no risk of negative influence on adjacent tissues, which occurs when procedures based on localized application of radio frequencies, ultrasound or laser signals.

Are the results definite?

Adipocids under the action of the device gradually release fats, and then are gradually absorbed by the process of natural metabolism and are completely eliminated from the body.

How is the process of destruction of adipocytes?

The basis of the procedure is the extraction of heat from adipose tissue. The device has a cooling effect on fat cells using a local and controlled procedure aimed at achieving in adipocytes a temperature below 10 Â° C for a certain period of time.

  • pregnancy and lactation;

  • violation of peripheral circulation;
  • infection and skin trauma;
  • areas of recent hemorrhage, bleeding;
  • excess obesity.
