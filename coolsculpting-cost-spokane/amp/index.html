General information

Recently, in the practice of aesthetic medicine, new methods of correction of the body without surgical intervention are being introduced more and more.

In 2008, American scientists supplemented the arsenal of cosmetologists with a new technique called cryolipolysis or coolsculpting.

The procedure of cryolipolysis has undergone comprehensive clinical trials. It is carried out in several stages with the help of specially designed devices.

What is the essence of coolsculpting treatment, indications and contraindications to this procedure, reviews of doctors and patients, duration and combination with other procedures, as well as before and after pictures and average prices for cryoliposuction – all this we learn later.

Mechanism of cold fat splitting

The procedure of coolsculpting is the decomposition of subcutaneous fat deposits under the influence of cold. Cooling the body starts the process of natural destruction of adipose tissue with the subsequent removal of decay products from the body. Fat cells are very sensitive to a decrease in temperature and after a supercooling are massively subjected to apoptosis. Fat splitting does not occur during cooling, but within 2 to 3 months after the procedure. The mechanism of this process is not fully understood, it is assumed that its main cause is the energy starvation of cells of another type – adipocytes.

Controlled Cooling That Effectively Destroys The Cells Without Harming The Skin Or Surrounding Tissue

Another hypothesis relates the cold splitting of lipids to their crystallization upon cooling. Anyway, the process of apoptosis leads to a decrease in the thickness of the fat layer by 20 – 25%. Unlike necrotic changes, apoptosis is more prolonged in time, proceeds without inflammatory reactions and scarring.

Advantages

Practically everyone has one or several problem zones on the body, where fat deposits practically can not be corrected by physiological methods. This can be the zone of thighs, buttocks, hips, above the knees.

The formation of local fat deposits may be due to hereditary factors or features of the functioning of the endocrine system and until recently the only effective method of controlling them was surgical liposuction.

Surgical liposuction is a traumatic intervention, it involves a significant burden on the body (anesthesia, drug treatment) and inconveniences. The patient has to make dressing, wear special clothing, limit physical activity. In addition, there are a number of contraindications to the operation.

CoolSculpting Appeals To Many Men And Women Who Have Never Undergone A Cosmetic Procedure

Coolsculpting has several advantages over surgical correction of the body:

  • As a result of coolsculpting treatment, the number of fat cells may be reduced by half.
  • The procedure is non-traumatic, after cryolipolysis, scars are not formed.
  • The cold effect is made without anesthesia and is well tolerated.
  • The fat cells destroyed during cryolipolysis are not restored.
  • After the procedure, there is no need to install lymph drainage and adhere to a special diet.
  • Coolsculpting is suitable for local correction of problem areas.
  • Special rehabilitation after lipolysis is not required.
  • The absence of an instantaneous result means that the decay products are removed gradually, without creating additional stress on the liver and excretory system.

Disadvantages

Even the most advanced technology is not without flaws and coolsculpting is no exception. Among the shortcomings of the coolsculpting procedure are:

CoolSculpting Is A Truly Innovative Procedure That Is Both Safe And Effective

  1. High price.
  2. Limited availability.
  3. The lack of quick results.
  4. The need to adhere to a certain diet and physical activity.
  5. Side effects.
  6. Cryolipolysis is ineffective on stretched skin.
  7. It is used exclusively locally.
  8. Cold impact is not suitable for correction of fat deposits in small areas of the body.

The need for a correction of the way of life can be attributed to shortcomings very conditionally, since this is one of the key conditions for the preservation of beauty and health in general.

The absence of instant visible effects is a clear drawback from the point of view of aesthetics, but is more physiological and does not pose a threat to the state of health.
