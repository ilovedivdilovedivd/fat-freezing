Which CoolSculpting Applicator To Treat Your Flanks?

The Cool Curve is the applicator generally used on the flanks. Each applicator treatment is about the size of your hand. Put your hand on the area you want treated. If that covers everything, one may be enough.

If the area you want treated is bigger than your hand, then, two applicators may be better. (Lauren Chavez, MD, Albuquerque Physician)

CoolSculpting Applicators

The type of applicator used depends on how it fits on you best. I have used both the curve as well as the core on people’s flanks before. I have also used the older applicators that take 1 hour per treatment since those are even curvier than the new curve.

It really depends on your body habitus. As for the number of applicators needed per flank, it again depends on your specific width. If the provider is experienced, they will know what is appropriate for you.

I have always found people to be happier when we meet their expectations and, in order, to do that 2 applicators may be needed. You should also see if they give discounts on a second session because every time you do CS you will get another 20-25% fat reduction.

Often, people’s expectations of what 1 session will do is actually what they get when they do 2 sessions. (Tianna Tsitsis, MD, Mount Vernon Physician)

The type, number and placement of applicators will vary depending on each individual patient, the specifics of which will be determined in the office during your assessment.

It is quite common to use overlapping handpiece placements (at separate sessions) in order to appropriately recruit tissues and treat different curves and high points.

Also, it is quite common to need more than one treatment (same location, same applicator) approx 6-8 weeks after the first treatment.
