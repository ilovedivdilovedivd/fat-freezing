What is Coolsculpting?

The principle of this method is very simple, but to understand it, it is necessary to imagine the reaction of the human body to cold and heat.

Numerous scientific studies have shown that fat cells are extremely susceptible to low temperatures and under the influence of the cold, they begin to break down.

Excess fat begins to split when the tissue is cooled to -5 Â° C. Immediately after reaching this threshold, the process of physiological apoptosis starts, i.e., the destruction of fat cells, which lasts for eight to twelve weeks after the end of the course of cryolipolysis.

In the end, the fat layer will decrease by an average of 30%.

Since the procedure is based on a natural way of removing fat from the body, it not only contributes to the loss of extra pounds, but also tones the skin and improves the overall condition of the body.

Under the influence of cold, the muscles contract and tighten the skin, so that losing weight does not cause stress in the body.

Device for cryolipolysis

The procedure can be carried out using two types of device:

Zeltiq device with vacuum nozzles. During the session, the fat fold is draw by the applicator using a vacuum. Cryolipolysis in this case lasts about an hour and, as a rule, leaves behind small bruises and swelling. Most often in the clinic this type of device is used.

Device with metal plates (CRYO-X). Metal plates cool the surface of the skin, muscles and internal organs are protected at the same time. The session takes 30 minutes, hematomas after it are not formed.

How is the cryolipolysis procedure carried out?

Carrying out of cryolipolysis can be divided into several stages.

Medical examination. At this stage, possible contraindications are identified and the affected areas are determined. In addition, the cost of the rate of cryolipolysis is calculated, which depends on how many zones need to be processed, on the type of device used and the size of the nozzle.

Preparation for cryolipolysis. If there are no contraindications, you can prepare for the procedure. To do this, a gel patch is placed on the treated area, designed to protect the skin from the cooling element in the applicator. Only after this the doctor passes to the installation of the vacuum nozzle.

Contraindications

Contraindications to coolsculpting are few:

  • pregnancy;
  • autoimmune diseases;
  • cold allergy;
  • infection and trauma in the proposed treatment area;
  • problems with blood vessels;
  • presence of a pacemaker;
  • Dermatitis, eczema or rash on the area of skin that is planned to be exposed.
