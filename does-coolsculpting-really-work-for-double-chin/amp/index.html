Does Coolsculpting Really Work For Double Chin?

Yes the chin is a very common area to be treated with Coolsculpting, as many find it hard to ‘work’ this area out 😉 The technique has been perfected by Zeltiqs outstanding technology with the Cool Mini applicator.

As for the face Coolsculpting is not done there. Below jaw line there are many options. Each patient is unique in how many treatments will be needed. Find a certified practice, ask to see their before and after pictures, and schedule a consult for your own treatment plan. (Jennifer Cova, DO, Dayton Physician)

CoolMini Removes Double Chins

CoolSculpting has a new applicator called the CoolMini that can eliminate double chins.

CoolSculpting freezes away up to 20% to 30% of fat at a time. Therefore, most patients may need two to three sessions before they get the results they seek.

Best Candidates For CoolMini Have An Adequate Amount Of Fat Beneath Their Chin

Usually prices are quoted after you have seen your doctor and they can inform you about what treatment will be best for you and how much it will cost. (Kimberly Butterwick, MD, San Diego Dermatologic Surgeon)

CoolMini has been out now since the end of 2015

Zeltiq released their CoolMini Applicator towards the end of 2015 and the applicator has been doing phenomenally well. The American Society of Dermatology conducted a study about a year ago which showed incredible interest in dealing with the treatment of this area as things like snapchat usage have really put selfies on the forefront of consumers minds.

There are currently 2 major minimally invasive options for treatment of the submental area both kybella and coolsculpting. Interstingly Allergan the company that owns Kybella was previously considering the acquistion of the coolsculpting technology prior to purchasing Kybella.

Cool Mini Can Give You A More Sculpted Submental Area

My honest belief is that they made a mistake in purchasing Kybella over coolsculpting as the Kybella treatments are more expensive, take longer to show efficacy and are said to be quite uncomfortable with a significant amount of swelling and discomfort after each of 3-6 treatment sessions. (Paul Vitenas, Jr., MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

CoolSculpting Cannot Manage Removing Neck Fat

Unfortunately, Coolsculpting is not effective in the management of neck fat. This is because the coolsculpting hand pieces are not small enough for use in the neck. At some point, new hand pieces may be available to address fat accumulation in the neck.

Until then, liposuction is probably the best therapeutic option. If you have concerns about localized fat collections in the neck area, consultation of a board certified plastic surgeon would be appropriate. This surgeon will be able to help you find an appropriate solution to your problem. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Coolsculpting has not been approved for the face. However, we have been told by the company that chin area is being investigated.
